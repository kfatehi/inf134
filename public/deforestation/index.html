DEFORESTATION

Our world is home to forests that cover 31% of Earth's land surface.

Not only are they home to 80 percent of the world's terrestrial biodiversity, but they are a major source of oxygen in the atmosphere.

300 million people call forests home and 1.6 billion depend on them to survive.

Forests also serve as places for recreational activity.

Do you like going camping, hiking, or biking in fresh air?

Well you are not alone. Millions of other people travel from different parts of the world to escape to the forests to de-stress.

Over the past 40 years, the rate of deforestation has significantly increased to satisfy the needs of the people.

Land is needed for cropland and infrastructures.

Wood is needed for fuel and paper.

But, when deforestation occurs, the effects lead to serious consequences.

SCROLL DOWN

When the slash and burn technique is used to clear land for agricultural reasons, massive amounts of carbon dioxide are released into the atmosphere, contributing to Climate Change.

SCROLL DOWN

Animals are homeless.

They are left with limited resources and no place to go.

With no accessible food or shelter, they have the risk of becoming endangered or even extinct.

SCROLL DOWN

Our world needs forests to absorb greenhouse gases, provide resources and shelter for millions of people and animals, and give people a place for recreational activities, to name a few.

There are many organizations that want to fight deforestation and educate people about the serious repercussions associated with it.

  • WWF started the Global Forest and Trade Network(GTFN)
  • Eyes on the Forest focuses on forest conservation and protecting the rights of people who rely on them.
  • Cosmetic company, L'oreal, is committed to "Zero Deforestation" by 2020.
