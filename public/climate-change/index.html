CLIMATE CHANGE

Have you noticed that summers are hotter and winters are warmer?

Do you ever see breaking news about an extreme weather event that threatens the places people call home?

Well, these events are occuring because of the rapid climate change occurring on Earth.

People sometimes ask, “What’s the difference between global warming and climate change?”

Well, global warming refers to the increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to rising levels of greenhouse gases while climate change refers to the broader set of changes that go along with global warming, including changes in weather patterns, the oceans, ice and snow, and ecosystems.

According to NASA, "97 percent of climate scientists agree that climate-warming trends over the past century are very likely due to human activities."

We rely on fossil fuels to power our cars, give us light in the dark, and produce goods that we use on a daily basis, just to name a few.

We have grown accustom to using large amounts of fossil fuels to help us get through daily activities, but we sometimes overlook the long-term effects it has on us and our environment.

The effects of climate change include:

  • Rising sea levels and temperatures
  • Changes in preciptation patterns
  • Stronger and more intense hurricanes
  • More droughts and heat waves

So What?


Well, 40% of the world's population lives within 100 kilometers of the coast.

By 2100, it is predicted that 1.9 million U.S. homes will be underwater due to the rise in sea levels.

Hurricanes can wipe out entire cities, as seen in 2005 when Hurricane Katrina struck the Gulf Coast.

More droughts mean less water for you to consume and use on a daily basis. No more long showers or green front lawns.

People and organizations around the world have made it their mission to combat climate change:

  • EPA educating younger generations about climate change.
  • NASA presents detailed evidence about the cause and effects of climate change.
  • One of the UN's sustainable development goals is to take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.
  • 28 countries signed the Paris Agreement that stated that they would work to limit global temperature rise.
