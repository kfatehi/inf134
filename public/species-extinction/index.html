SPECIES EXTINCTION

Earth, home to about 8.7 million species.

These 8.7 million documented species include all animals and plants alike.

They also play a major role in ensuring the resilence of the various ecosystems.

While extinction is considered a natural phenomenon that occurs in the background, Our planet is experiencing a huge wave of extinction in both plants and animals.

In our current state, scientist are estimating that we are now losing about 1,000 to 10,000 times the normal rate of extinction.

Most of the past waves of extinctions were caused by events such as volcanic eruptions, asteroid strikes or natural climate shifts.

However, the current crisis is considered to be caused by none other than us, humans.

The introduction of developing socities created more human activies which in turn introduced more exotic species but also drove animals out of their normal habitats as well as created the global warming crisis.

There may not be clear estimate as to how many species are in actual danger of becoming extinct.

It is very clear how big of an issue species extinction really is as well as the increasing numbers of species that are at risk of possibly disappearing forever.

There are lots of people and organizations working towards discovering which species are heading towards extinction.
